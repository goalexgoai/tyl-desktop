Send texts individually, not as a group

Group texts are convenient — but they're also impersonal. When someone sees a group thread pop up on their phone, they know immediately they're one of dozens. Response rates fall. Messages get ignored.

There's a better way: send each person their own private text from your real number. That's what Text Your List does.

The problem with group texts

Group texts create friction in every direction. Replies go to everyone, which means one off-topic response derails the whole thread. People feel less inclined to respond at all because it feels like a broadcast, not a conversation. And you can't personalize anything — everyone gets the exact same message.

If you're reaching out to customers, clients, congregation members, or your team, a group text signals that you didn't take the time to reach out individually. Even if the content is the same, the medium tells a story.

How Text Your List works

You upload a CSV of contacts, write one message template, and Text Your List sends a separate private text to each person from your real phone number. No shortcode. No shared sender. No group thread. Each recipient gets a message that looks like you sent it just to them — because you did, just with some help.

Replies come back to your normal messages app, not a dashboard. Conversations stay one-on-one. And you can personalize every message with the recipient's first name or any other field from your spreadsheet.

Who uses it

Real estate agents staying in touch with their sphere. Coaches sending game-day reminders. Church coordinators reaching their congregation. Small business owners following up with customers. Anyone who has a list and wants each person on it to feel like the only person on it.
